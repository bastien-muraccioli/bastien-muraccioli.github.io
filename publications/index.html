2022

  1. Can robots be good public speakers?
    Gentiane Venture, Bastien Muraccioli, Marie-Luce Bourguet, and 1 more author
    In Proceedings of the Sixteenth International Conference on Tangible, Embedded, and Embodied Interaction, Daejeon, Republic of Korea, Apr 2022
    Abs DOI

    Our research aims at understanding if robots could be good at public speaking, what they need to achieve the level of a good public speaker and how they may surpass a human public speaker. Previous research results indicate that designing a robot speaker by mimicking some of the behaviours of a human speaker is not enough to create an effective robot speech performance. It can in fact be counter productive to strive for human-likeliness. In this paper, we describe how we programmed a toy-like, non-anthropomorphic small robot (the Anki Vector robot) to deliver a speech by extracting pose and facial expression information from the video of a human speaker and loosely retargeting this information to the robot. We also describe our experimental plans to compare Vector’s speech delivery performance with the performance of a more anthropomorphic robot (the SoftBank Pepper robot), which has been programmed to closely mimic the human speaker’s behaviour. They are compared in terms of their ability to evoke the positive affective responses necessary to spark interest, motivate the audience to listen, and engage the audience in meaningful ways.
